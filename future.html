Delegated Closure

A new, falsifiable measurement theory of how Q drops work — and the research program that would test it. The producer/consumer "guided apophenia" reading is not new (Berkowitz, Argentino, Phillips & Milner). What is new is the Delegated-Closure Index (DCI): the first per-post, embedding-free, LLM-free, regex-computable measure of how much interpretive closure a drop withholds — built to be non-circular and tested against its own pre-registered falsifiers, negatives and all.

This page is deliberately written like a pre-registration, not a press release. 1

Delegated Closure — a measurement, not a new story

The concept is borrowed and credited; the contribution is a number

Thesis. Q drops do not transmit cult control; they solicit it by withholding interpretive closure, so the audience self-authors the missing meaning and experiences it as personal discovery rather than imposed dogma. The producer leaves a gap; the consumer fills it; ownership of the conclusion transfers to the reader.

That division of labor is the prior consensus reading of QAnon — Reed Berkowitz's "guided apophenia," Marc-André Argentino's QAnon-as-ARG, Phillips & Milner's "participatory disinformation," Muirhead & Rosenblum's "conspiracy without the theory." We do not claim it as new. Our contribution is purely a measurement one: the first per-post index that turns "gap-leaving" from a qualitative observation into a reproducible number you can compute, ablate, and falsify.

Under predictive processing, a drop is salient because it raises prediction-error (a question, a redaction, a decode-prompt) but supplies no posterior. The loop is left open; the reader closes it by pattern-completion; because the resolution is self-generated, it is held as identity-owned truth.

The five-step mechanism: (1) under-specify at source → (2) raise prediction-error without resolving it → (3) hand off the inferential labor via decode-operators → (4) the audience self-authors closure through apophenia → (5) economize on falsifiability (dated claims self-extinguish on failure, so they are selected out). Step 5 is the only place we attempt a genuinely novel, measurable claim — and, as the scorecard shows, the corpus did not support it.

The Delegated-Closure Index (DCI)

Six lexical components · weights sum to 1 · computed by dci.py

DCI is a per-drop score in [0,1]. Higher = the drop raises interpretive demand (questions, redaction, decode-prompts, brevity, no dated claim, no external grounding) while supplying no answer of its own. Engagement is never an input, and it reads only three posts.json fields: text, referenced_posts, and post_metadata.time.

DCIi = clip(  0.22·INTERROGATIVE  + 0.20·DECODE  + 0.18·REDACTION  + 0.15·BREVITY  + 0.13·(1−CONCRETENESS)  + 0.12·(1−GROUNDING),  0, 1 )

3

Why DCI is non-circular

Measurement-independent of BITE — proven, not asserted

A metric that secretly re-counts the thing it predicts proves nothing. DCI was hardened against three circularity attacks from the red-team:

  1. 1 · Engagement is never an input

    DCI reads only the drop's own text/metadata. Amplification, replies, and reach are excluded by construction, so DCI cannot "predict" engagement by containing it.

  2. 2 · BITE-shared tokens removed from DECODE

    The phrases future proves past, trust the plan, and the map are scored by bite_scorer.py (lines 104/124/105). They are deliberately excluded from DCI's DECODE lexicon, so DCI and BITE never count the same string under opposite labels.

  3. 3 · Word-count partialled out

    Because brevity inflates DCI and deflates any keyword density, every DCI-vs-BITE comparison removes word_count first, so a brevity coincidence can't masquerade as a finding.

Empirical result (computed by dci.py): the DECODE↔BITE lexicon overlap is 0 tokens (now computed, not asserted); correlation of DCI with BITE-total = 0.034; partial correlation controlling word-count = −0.054. DCI and BITE are statistically independent.
Caveat reported, not hidden: per-drop BITE-Thought variance is only 0.0065 (mean 0.0303) — a near-floor — so any claim that "DCI out-predicts BITE-T" would be uninformative, and we withdraw it.

Openness "drift" and the falsifiability economy

The honest negatives — and why the per-component view is more interesting than the headline

Openness Drift Slope (ODS). We regressed DCI on posting time. The overall slope is +0.0012/yr, and a 1,000-iteration permutation test gives p=0.54 — fully indistinguishable from noise. Composite DCI does not trend. But the per-component breakout (the test the red-team demanded) tells a sharper story: the drops shifted mode of openness rather than raising its level.

Reading it. Late drops got shorter and more redacted (more open), but also more externally grounded via links and less interrogative / decode-prompted (less open). Early QAnon delegated through Socratic question-cascades; late QAnon delegated through brevity and blackout while offloading to outside links. The net is flat — so the clean "openness rises over time" narrative is downgraded to a mode-shift, not a magnitude increase. That is a more defensible — and more interesting — claim than the one we set out to make.

Falsifiability Economy Ratio (FER) = decode-share ÷ concrete-share, 2017–19. The theory predicted FER would rise (deferral retained, concreteness culled). It falls: 0.712 → 0.497 → 0.337, because explicit decode-prompts thinned faster than dated claims did. By its own pre-registered falsifier, the falsifiability-economy mechanism is rejected on this corpus. The 2020 concreteness rebound (and a small-n 2022 spike) were disclosed and excluded by the stated 2017–19 cutoff, not used as support.

5

Robustness & supporting metrics

Weight-invariance, leave-one-out, and the discriminant test

  • Weight invariance. Re-scoring with uniform 1/6 weights correlates with the weighted DCI at r=0.96, preserves the year-ordering of means, and keeps the ODS sign (−0.004/yr). The specific weights are not load-bearing — a stated requirement before any publication.
  • Leave-one-component-out. Dropping any single component and renormalizing leaves the distribution and drift sign stable; no component alone drives DCI (each ablation correlates r≈0.9+ with the full index).
  • Discriminant validity. Constructed/located vague-but-self-resolving texts (long, declarative, URL-grounded, dated) score low DCI despite semantic fuzziness, while short interrogative redacted drops score high — so DCI is not "vagueness re-weighted."
  • Still open (Phase 1 deliverable): a PCA-from-loadings weight justification and a 100-drop human-coded convergent-validity study with inter-rater κ. Until that lands, DCI is an index of regex incidence with strong face validity, not yet a κ-validated measure of interpretive debt. Stated, not hidden.
▲

Data-integrity disclosure: the downstream corpora are synthetic

The single most important result on this page

The "5.6–7.1× amplification" figure is an artifact, not evidence. While building this theory, an adversarial verification step inspected the data-collection code and catalog. collect_datasets.py (the make_sample generator, lines 353–375) fabricates all six downstream datasets — Reddit, 4chan, 8kun, Parler, Telegram, Twitter — by cycling a handful of Q-saturated seed sentences with slogan suffixes under a fixed RNG seed. datasets/catalog.json confirms it: every downstream dataset has local_path: null and access: manual/api.

So the earlier finding that "downstream platforms score 5.6–7.1× the drops' BITE-total" is researcher-authored prose scored by a Q-keyword lexicon — a tautology, not audience behavior. The Results page and home page now carry this caveat. No source→downstream claim in this theory is computable until real, provenance-documented platform corpora replace the seed prose.

This is why the co-production gradient (the most ambitious part of Delegated Closure) is presented as pre-registered and blocked, not as a finding. It also defines Phase 0 of the roadmap below as a hard dependency for everything cross-platform.

Research roadmap — future steps

Five phases, with Phase 0 a hard data-integrity gate on everything cross-platform

  1. Phase 0
    GATE

    Replace the synthetic downstream corpora with real, provenance-documented data

    Collect Reddit/4chan/8kun/Parler/Telegram/Twitter QAnon corpora from PushShift / 4plebs / academic-torrents with collection dates, sampling frame, and confidence intervals; update catalog.json with real local_paths. Until this lands, no source→downstream claim is computable and the 5.6–7.1× figure stays flagged as artifact.

    Deliverable · provenance-documented platform corpora + updated catalog
  2. Phase 1

    Lock & validate the non-circular metric

    Freeze dci.py (BITE-disjoint DECODE, purely-lexical REDACTION/GROUNDING). Add a PCA-from-loadings weight justification, leave-one-out and uniform-weight re-reports, and a 100-drop human-coded convergent-validity study with inter-rater κ.

    Deliverable · frozen dci.py, ablation/PCA tables, κ-validated human comparison
  3. Phase 2

    Within-source falsification battery (computable now)

    The placebo-controlled, word-count-partialled source-only tests — ODS overall + per-component, FER over 2017–19, DCI-vs-BITE partial correlation, and the discriminant test. Already run here; Phase 2 hardens them with confidence intervals and the human-rating cross-check.

    Deliverable · a results table stating each falsifier and whether the run cleared it
  4. Phase 3

    Cross-source mediation with slogan-ablated BITE (gated on Phase 0)

    On real data, regress per-platform BITE-T amplification on source DCI vs source BITE-T within length-and-slogan-matched strata, with WWG1WGA / "the storm" / "great awakening" ablated. Pre-registered falsifier: if source BITE-T predicts downstream control at partial-r ≥ source DCI, delegation is rejected. Add a non-lexicon outcome (reply-depth / anchor-word reuse) so the result isn't internal to the dictionary.

    Deliverable · mediation coefficients + CIs, slogan-ablated gradient, non-lexicon validation
  5. Phase 4

    Publication as a measurement contribution with an honest ledger

    Frame the write-up as "the first computable operationalization of guided apophenia," crediting Berkowitz / Argentino / Phillips & Milner / Muirhead & Rosenblum up front, presenting DCI + the falsification battery + either the real-data gradient or its explicit deferral.

    Deliverable · manuscript + reproducible repo + this page

Reproduce everything on this page: python dci.py → results/improved/dci_findings.json. The metric reads only the real Q-drop corpus; downstream platforms are intentionally excluded until Phase 0 is met.

Why include the negatives? Because the whole dossier grades claims by evidence, and a theory page that buried its rejected hypotheses and its synthetic-data problem would violate that contract. Delegated Closure ships a validated, non-circular metric and an honest account of what it could and could not yet show. The most valuable output is the question it forces: collect real downstream data, then ask whether source openness predicts the control the audience manufactures.
