Key Findings

BITE Model Scores by Platform

Each platform scored on Hassan's four-dimension BITE model using a keyword-weighted NLP lexicon. Scores are mean across all posts in the dataset (0 = absent → 1 = pervasive). The dominant dimension is highlighted.

⚠ Construct-validity caveat (post-audit). This BITE scorer is an unvalidated hand-built keyword lexicon — no human coding, no inter-rater reliability — and it has no stance/negation handling, so a sentence that debunks QAnon scores as "control" purely from containing the terms. It measures topic salience, not coercion. And six of the seven datasets here are synthetic, so all cross-platform comparisons (including the "amplification" gradient) are illustrative, not evidence. See the Methods Audit.

Improved Experiment — Ablation & Best Result BERTopic · T4 · 12 runs

The refreshed run (improved_experiment.py) re-clusters the Q corpus (4,248 drops after empty/duplicate removal; 4,135 with the admin filter — not the full 4,966) across an ablation grid: three embedding models × min_cluster_size {15, 25, 40}, in full and admin-filtered corpora. Each run is scored by 0.50·persistence + 0.30·silhouette + 0.20·coherence.

⚠ Read with care (post-audit). Persistence is the heaviest term and it mechanically rewards coarse few-topic splits (r≈−0.99 with topic count); the silhouette is computed in the UMAP space HDBSCAN optimised (an internal sanity check, not external validity); and the winner is a single max over many runs with no confidence intervals. The "2 robust topics" result is a property of this scoring. See the Methods Audit.

Methodology

Pipeline: collect_datasets.py → multi_dataset_analysis.py → compare_results.py

Clustering: Method is loaded from run metadata (kmeans). For Q drops the full corpus is used when available; other datasets may be representative samples depending on data access.

BITE scoring: Keyword-weighted lexicon (136 patterns across B/I/T/E dimensions) with sigmoid saturation. Implementation: bite_scorer.py.

Topic overlap (Hoseini et al. 2021): Jaccard similarity of the union of top-10 keywords per cluster, compared across all 21 platform pairs.

Motivated reasoning (Priniski & Bavel 2021): Keyword classifier across four categories: certainty, us-vs-them framing, dismissal of outsiders, in-group solidarity.

Platform migration (iDRAMA Lab): Monthly post counts per platform field in the normalised schema.

Limitations: Sample availability varies by platform and affects comparability. See datasets/CATALOG.md for full-access instructions. Interpret cross-platform magnitude differences as directional unless all datasets are fully downloaded and normalized from raw corpora.
