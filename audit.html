Methods Audit

A critical review of this project by this project. Thirteen agents across six research-standard dimensions found, then adversarially verified, 35 flaws — from fabricated downstream data to a mislabeled scoring formula. 13 were corrected this pass and have been moved to the changelog; the tables below now carry only the 13 live limitations that still constrain how the work should be read.

The dossier's contract is to grade every claim by evidence. This page applies that contract to the dossier's own methods. Findings that were fixed in code or on the pages have been removed from the live tables and relocated to the Resolved changelog, so what remains in view is only what is still Mitigated, Documented, or Open — the synthetic-data problem, the unvalidated lexicons, and the circular silhouette among them. Nothing is deleted; the corrected items are preserved below with their evidence.

Found by severity: 4 critical · 15 high · 11 medium · 5 low. Of the highest-impact items triaged here, 13 were corrected (now in the changelog) and 13 remain live — the 4 critical below plus 9 high/medium/low that are Mitigated, Documented, or Open.

Overall assessment. The conclusions split sharply by data source. The descriptive analysis of the real 4,966-drop Q corpus — that the drops are an affect-heavy, vague, low-concreteness stream on a US-timezone clock — is broadly supported, reproducible from the committed JSON, and often honestly caveated. But every cross-platform and "replication" conclusion is unsupported as presented: six of seven datasets are fabricated from a handful of Q-saturated templates, the iDRAMA "diaspora" merely echoes hard-coded timestamps, and the replication badges are mechanical presence checks. The two custom "cult-control" instruments (BITE, motivated reasoning) are unvalidated keyword lexicons with no stance handling, so they conflate topic mention with coercion. The statistics are exploratory: no CIs or multiple-comparison control, a mislabeled persistence-dominant composite, and a circular UMAP-space silhouette propping up the "2 robust topics" headline. Net: a transparent, partially self-critical exploratory dossier whose real-corpus descriptive claims are defensible but whose cross-platform, replication, and cult-quantification claims should be re-scoped before being presented as findings.

How the audit was run

Find → adversarially verify → synthesize, so the critique meets its own standard

Six dimensions — statistical validity, construct/measurement validity, data provenance, replication, code correctness, and interpretation/ethics — were each handed to a "finder" agent that read the actual files and cited file:line. Every finding then went to an independent adversarial verifier that re-opened the cited evidence and could confirm, downgrade, or refute it (a critique that is itself wrong is worse than none). A final agent synthesized only the non-refuted findings. Thirteen agents, ~575k tokens. The most load-bearing numbers were also re-derived by hand from the committed artifacts.

This is the same discipline the Delegated-Closure page used on its own theory — applied here to the whole project.

Where the changes landed, by file

  • dci.pyRemoved the acronym-confounded ALLCAPS signal from REDACTION; replaced the eyeballed placebo with a 1,000-iteration two-sided permutation p-value; replaced the hard-coded "0 BITE overlap" with a computed check. Re-ran → DCI mean 0.342→0.329, openness-drift p=0.54.
  • bite_scorer.pyFixed the even-n median (now statistics.median); added a prominent construct-validity + no-stance-handling limitations block to the docstring.
  • multi_dataset_analysis.pyAdded stopword + digit filtering to the topic-overlap keyword sets (de-inflating the Hoseini Jaccard); seeded the multi-dataset BERTopic's UMAP for reproducibility; documented the real/synthetic migration conflation.
  • qdrops_cluster.pyLabelled the silhouette as a UMAP-space internal sanity check, not external validity.
  • datasets/catalog.jsonCorrected the Q-drop date range to 2017-10-28 → 2022-11-28 and noted the third-party-archive limitations.
  • Home / Results / Future / READMECorrected the composite formula (0.50/0.30/0.20), the analyzed N (4,248/4,135), the circadian and single-operator framing, the cross-platform synthetic caveats, the BITE construct-validity disclaimer, the replication-label caveat, and all updated DCI numbers.

What is not fixed here, by honesty: collecting real downstream data, a κ-validated human-coding study of BITE, full bootstrap CIs and multiple-comparison control across the grid, and a pre-UMAP silhouette. These need new data or new studies and are tracked as Open / Phase-0 on the roadmap.

What holds up

A fair audit credits the calibrated parts too

  • The real-corpus descriptive work is grounded. The circadian, style, BITE-on-Q, and DCI analyses run on the actual 4,966 drops and recompute exactly from the committed JSON.
  • Unusual transparency. The code self-documents its gaps (no_confidence_reporting:true; the DCI page discloses its missing κ), which is why verification was fast — good-faith reporting, not concealment.
  • Several headlines were already calibrated. The Future page reports its 2-of-3 hypothesis failures and calls the synthetic-data disclosure "the most important result"; the amplification number was already flagged provisional.
  • DCI was deliberately built non-circular. It excludes BITE tokens and partials word-count — awareness of construct contamination, now verified (0-token overlap, r≈0.03).
  • The interpretive instruments carry humility. The dossier already calls the Lifton/BITE scores "interpretive instruments, not measured quantities."

The point of publishing this. A project that analyses a conspiracy movement by computational means is only as trustworthy as its willingness to turn the same scrutiny on itself. The descriptive Q-corpus findings survive that scrutiny; the cross-platform and cult-quantification claims do not, and are now marked as such throughout. The honest path forward is the roadmap, not louder claims.
