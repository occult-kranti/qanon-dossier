The QAnon Dossier

A self-contained, source-grounded study of QAnon as a documented political movement and digital cult — paired with a reproducible lab that turns all ~4,950 "Q drops" into measurable data. This page is the map: what each section is for, and how every analysis was built and what it can (and can't) show.

QAnon is treated here as an object of study, not a set of claims to be entertained. Every load-bearing statement carries an epistemic tag (see the rail), debunked assertions are flagged with rebuttals, and the conspiracy's own texts appear only as primary-source discourse objects for analysis. The aim is calibration: to say clearly what is documented, what is contested, and what is false — and, in the computational sections, to measure structure in the corpus without ever treating the maths as a verdict on truth.

Patterns & commonalities across the drops

What recurs — in the text, across platforms, and in the posting metadata

Three independent lenses agree on one thing: the corpus is far more uniform than its 4,966 drops suggest. A reproducible metadata pass (qdrops_patterns.py → results/improved/original_findings.json) surfaces commonalities the topic/BITE pipeline discards.

  1. A · Shared cross-platform lexicon

    One vocabulary travels everywhere

    Across all 21 platform pairs, the same anchor words recur regardless of which two platforms are compared: storm, patriots, coming, god, fisa, drop, msm, ready, wwg1wga, panic, map, trust, everything. The strongest pair is Parler × Twitter (Jaccard 0.273); the slogan WWG1WGA is the single most stable topic across every embedding model. ⚠ Caveat (post-audit): every cross-platform number here (the 21 pairs, the 0.273, the "shared lexicon") is computed over synthetic downstream samples, and the Jaccard was stopword-inflated (~27% function words; now fixed in code). Treat the cross-platform pattern as illustration, not evidence — see the Methods Audit. The WWG1WGA stability is on the real Q corpus and stands.

  2. B · Affective uniformity under clustering

    The corpus collapses to ~2 robust topics

    At the cluster size that maximises stability (mcs40), two independent models both resolve the corpus into just two topics — a WWG1WGA slogan cluster and a ~4,000-drop "god / patriots / time" affect blob — and they agree at Jaccard 0.928. Finer settings shatter into 40–56 fragile topics that don't survive a model swap. The stable structure is affective, not informational.

  3. C · Stylistic drift across the platform migration

    From Socratic essays to link-forwards

    As the drops moved 4chan → 8chan → 8kun, their form changed monotonically: median length fell 17 → 12 → 8 words, the share carrying a URL rose 2.8% → 31% → 56%, and questions per drop fell 3.22 → 1.21 → 1.20. The early corpus asks; the late corpus forwards links.

D · A US-timezone posting clock

Posting time-of-day (UTC, all 4,966 drops) shows a sharp nocturnal trough: the deepest three-hour window (08–13 UTC band; 11–13 UTC is the very bottom) contains under 0.5% of the corpus, and it lands in the human sleep band under every US time zone (≈03:00 PST → 06:00 EST). Caveat (post-audit): the headline "143× busy/quiet" ratio divides by a single-hour count of just n=3 drops — an unstable denominator; the windowed contrast is the robust statistic. And this shows a US-timezone schedule, which does not establish a single author — a team sharing US hours fits equally, consistent with the Critical Review's multiple-author finding.

Most backed by data

QAnon's power lives in interpretation and emotion, not instructions — and most of the "cult-control" signal is manufactured downstream by followers, not by Q.

Most conspiratorial · not endorsed

"The drops are a coordinated military-intelligence comms channel (the 'Q clock')"

The believer's reading points at the very same artifacts and sees authentication codes, synced timestamps, and a chain of command.

  • "Operational" clusters look like op-orders.sec_test · dod_route_20 · defend_b720 · auth_hold_rel_good; placeholder · oig · indictments · tracking; trip · tripcode · compromised.
  • "Q clock" timestamp sync.timestamp · past · future · proves; map · unlocks · stringer; potus · tweet · relevant — claimed to align drops to Trump tweets.

Why it fails. Every pillar is unfalsifiable post-hoc pattern-matching: timestamp "proofs" mine an unbounded space; stable slogans are repetition, not coordination; lock-step migration is deplatforming, not orders; the 2020-03 spike is the whole internet discovering COVID. Tellingly, the conspiracy reading requires exactly the open-ended vagueness the backed theory says was engineered.

The decisive contrast. Both columns cite the same tables. But the backed reading predicts and explains the conspiratorial one: a corpus engineered for reinterpretation (short, link-heavy, question-led, slogan-stable) is precisely a corpus in which motivated readers will always "find" a hidden signal. The data doesn't adjudicate Q's claims — it shows why the claims feel confirmable while remaining unfalsifiable.

My own QDrop theories — and experiments to test them

Four falsifiable hypotheses derived from the data, each with a designed test and a kill condition

These go beyond the existing pipeline. Each is stated as a falsifiable hypothesis with the evidence that motivates it, a concrete experiment, and the result that would disprove it — the discipline the Critical Review demands.

  1. T1US-timezone posting clock (authorship unresolved)

    The posting rhythm follows a single US-timezone human schedule — inconsistent with a 24/7 automated operation, though it does not by itself prove a single author (a US-hours team fits too).

    Evidence
    Deepest 3 h (08–13 UTC) = <0.5% of all drops; trough in the sleep band for every US zone; 25.6% of drops <5 min apart. (The "143×" single-hour ratio rests on n=3 and is reported as fragile.)
    Experiment
    Per-phase UTC-hour KDE (4ch / 8ch / 8kun) + a DST-shift test: does the trough move with US daylight-saving dates? Bootstrap a uniform null.
    Predicts
    One stable trough that shifts ≤1–2 h at the 8kun handoff and tracks US DST.
    Falsified if
    Activity is ~uniform across 24 h, or the trough ignores DST → consistent with automation / a distributed team.

  2. T2A stylometric handoff at the board migration

    Drop authorship/voice changes at the 8chan → 8kun boundary (Nov 2019), matching the documented Watkins-era transition — a style break, not a smooth evolution.

    Evidence
    Across phases, median words 17→12→8, URL share 2.8%→31%→56%, questions/drop 3.22→1.21→1.20; topic mix flips from essays to link-forwards.
    Experiment
    Change-point detection (PELT/Bayesian) on a monthly style vector {q-rate, length, URL share, we/our rate}; train a phase-A vs phase-C authorship classifier, score cross-phase separability.
    Predicts
    A significant change-point clustered at the migration boundary; high cross-phase classifier separability.
    Falsified if
    Change-points fall at random months, or A/C drops are not linearly separable → continuity of voice.

  3. T3Engagement-maximising vagueness ("falsifiability decay")

    Drops became deliberately less checkable over time — concreteness fell as the audience grew and as dated predictions failed.

    Evidence
    Action-prediction share 7.8%→3%; short-drop share 21.6%→39.6%; the famous dated "HRC arrest" prediction is drop #1 — and failed.
    Experiment
    Regress per-drop concreteness (named entities + explicit dates + declarative:question ratio) on time and cumulative downstream audience (migration volume); interrupted time-series around known failed-date events.
    Predicts
    Concreteness declines and steepens just after high-profile failed predictions.
    Falsified if
    Concreteness is flat or rises over time, or shows no break at failure events.

  4. T4Affective floor, informational ceiling

    A thin layer of real information topics (FISA, Epstein, China) floats on a thick affective substrate that absorbs most drops — which is why coarse clustering collapses to two topics.

    Evidence
    mcs40 → 2 topics (silhouette 0.79–0.82, cross-model Jaccard 0.928); the only stable/borderline anchors are WWG1WGA + a 4,000-drop "god/important/time" blob; admin-filtering barely changes it.
    Experiment
    Hierarchical persistence: for each drop, does its topic survive across all 3 embedding models? Report the % absorbed by the slogan/affect supercluster vs. genuine info topics; train an affect-vs-info classifier and track class balance over time.
    Predicts
    A large majority of drops live in non-persistent/affect clusters; info clusters are small, real, and front-loaded to 2018.
    Falsified if
    Many mid-sized information topics persist across all models → the corpus is genuinely information-rich.

All four reuse the existing stack — qdrops_patterns.py already emits the metadata features (T1–T3) and qdrops_sweep.py the persistence machinery (T4); each is a small, self-contained add-on, not a new pipeline.

What this is — and the rules it follows

Document and analyse; never amplify or endorse

Purpose & ethics. This project documents and analyses QAnon for academic, journalistic, and educational use. It does not promote its beliefs. Conspiracy assertions are labelled as such; debunked claims (e.g. the blood-libel-descended "adrenochrome" myth) are marked and rebutted; the drop texts are reproduced only as artifacts for analysis, with referenced images deliberately excluded; and survivor-support framing is carried throughout. The computational pages add a second discipline on top of this: every clustering result ships with validity numbers and a stability check, and is explicitly bounded by what the method cannot see.

Two ideas run through everything. First, the epistemic tags in the rail: each claim is graded from Documented down to Conspiracy, so the reader always knows the evidential status of a statement. Second, calibration over certainty: where credible sources disagree (how large is the real-world threat? who actually wrote the drops?), the dossier presents the disagreement rather than resolving it by fiat — most sharply on the Critical Review page.

How the drop analysis was built

Each component, what it does, and where it lives

The Drops lab is two Python scripts plus an interactive page. qdrops_cluster.py turns the raw archive into one analysed dataset; qdrops_sweep.py runs many configurations and scores their agreement; drops.html reads the output and renders it. Here is each piece.

  1. 1 · Topic clustering — letting themes self-form

    Embeddings → UMAP → HDBSCAN (not k-NN)

    Each drop is converted into a sentence-embedding vector that captures meaning, not just keywords. The vectors are compressed with UMAP and grouped with HDBSCAN, a density-based clusterer that chooses the number of topics itself and sets genuinely odd drops aside as "noise." BERTopic wraps this and labels each topic by its most distinctive words (class-based TF-IDF, with stopwords removed so labels read like "covid 19 virus lockdown," not "the of to"). Why not k-NN? k-NN is a supervised classifier — it needs predefined labels and so cannot discover topics; it only enters clustering legitimately as a nearest-neighbour graph fed to community detection. A plain TF-IDF + KMeans baseline is also included for comparison.

  2. 2 · Validity — measuring whether the clustering is any good

    Silhouette + c_v topic coherence

    "Good clustering" is measured, not eyeballed. Every run reports a silhouette score (how cleanly separated the clusters are, −1…1) and a c_v topic-coherence score from gensim (how interpretable each topic's words are, 0…1), both overall and per topic. Crucially, these are validity, not truth: a high score means the algorithm grouped the text well — it says nothing about whether any claim in the drops is correct.

  3. 3 · Stability — trusting only the topics that persist

    Multi-run sweep + membership-overlap (Jaccard)

    Clustering is parameter-sensitive: change the embedding model or the minimum cluster size and the topics shift. So the sweep runs several configurations (e.g. MiniLM vs. MPNet × min-cluster-size 15/25/40), embedding once per model for speed. Because every run clusters the same drops, topics can be matched across runs by document-membership overlap (Jaccard). A topic's persistence is its mean best-overlap with the other runs: stable (≥0.50) topics are probably real; fragile (<0.30) ones are likely artifacts of the settings. On the Drops page you can click any topic in the Stability panel to read its drops in the reader.

  4. 4 · Metadata mining — the fields most analyses discard

    Composition · board migration · tripcode rotations

    Beyond the text, each post carries structure worth charting. Composition shows how much of the corpus is actually prose vs. image-only or bare references — a quantitative footprint of vagueness. Board migration plots where drops were posted, by year, revealing the 4chan → 8chan → 8kun platform diaspora the literature tracks. And the tripcode-rotation timeline shows each cryptographic signature Q posted under and when it changed: rotations are documented authenticity fault-lines (every handoff is a point where account control could change), and secure "!!" tripcodes are distinguished from the brute-forceable single-"!" ones.

  5. 5 · Administrative tagging — separating ops from ideology

    Heuristic operational-drop filter

    A large share of drops are operational, not ideological — "test," "trip update," "comms," "disregard spelling," board/config messages. A rule-based tagger flags these so they can be excluded at clustering time (--drop-admin) or hidden in the reader, sharpening the thematic picture. It's a transparent heuristic, not a classifier, and is labelled as such.

  6. 6 · Cross-reference — triangulating against the scholarship

    This corpus vs. the peer-reviewed findings

    The honest move is to check the computed results against independent work and to be explicit about blind spots. A table on the Drops page lines up this corpus's measured facts with published conclusions — the Gospel/iDRAMA paper, de Zeeuw's normiefication study, Hoseini's Telegram analysis, Priniski's good-vs-evil mental-model paper — and marks each Consistent, Adjacent, Out-of-scope, or Needs-stylometry. That calibration — saying what the method can and cannot see — is the analytical payload.

Data & reproducibility

(2025), JSON-QAnon, DOI 10.13140/RG.2.2.28778.32964. The drops are reproduced here strictly as primary-source artifacts; referenced images are excluded.

The whole lab is reproducible from the command line. The pipeline downloads the dataset automatically:

How to read the analytics — and what they can't show

The same calibration the Critical Review argues, applied to the maths

Three cautions travel with every chart in the Drops lab:

  1. Validity is not truth

    Silhouette and coherence measure whether the algorithm grouped the text sensibly. They are wholly silent on whether any drop's claims are accurate. A crisp, coherent "the storm / arrests" cluster says people repeated that theme — not that arrests occurred.

  2. Topics are provisional

    Cluster boundaries move with the embedding model, the minimum cluster size, and UMAP settings. That is exactly why the Stability panel exists: treat a topic as real only if it persists across configurations, and read the noise share as a measure of how much of the corpus resists grouping at all.

  3. The method has blind spots

    Clustering measures themes and timing. It cannot establish authorship (that needs stylometry — see the "who is Q" debate on the Critical Review), it does not measure toxicity (that needs a trained classifier), and it cannot see the movement's non-English, off-platform life (the Telegram/global corpora). Findings outside its reach are flagged, not fudged.

Read this way, the lab's value is not a single answer but a structured, checkable reading of the corpus — one that states its own confidence and its own limits, in the same spirit as the rest of the dossier.

One project, two disciplines. The written pages grade every claim by evidence; the computational pages grade every result by validity and stability. Neither treats QAnon's assertions as true. Together they aim to model what careful, transparent analysis of a conspiracy movement looks like — documenting and explaining it without ever amplifying it.
